Chapter 7: Torn Pages and New Chapters

Tensions weren’t completely gone, of course. Governments remained skeptical, and news outlets churned out stories warning of potential propaganda or hidden influences. Still, Douyin continued to thrive, especially among young people who found themselves more interested in each other’s daily realities than in global conflicts.

James and Lin’s data project began to attract wider attention, eventually leading to a combined academic conference, held virtually, with participants from the U.S. and China. Sierra and Yao signed on as guest speakers to talk about building empathy through social media. Their livestream attracted a record-breaking audience, watchers from both nations flooding the chat with emojis, questions, and exclamations of solidarity.
