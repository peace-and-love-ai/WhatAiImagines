Chapter 4: The Surprising Backlash

Not everyone was pleased. Online forums flared up with debates over data privacy, cultural authenticity, censorship, and complicated geopolitics. Some Americans were suspicious: “They’re brainwashing our kids,” they claimed. Meanwhile, a few Chinese netizens wondered if Douyin might lose its cultural flavor: “We’re letting too many foreign voices shape our trends.”

Even so, the vast majority found in Douyin a kind of ongoing experiment in digital exchange. Some watchers questioned how certain sensitive topics would be handled. Would Douyin moderate them? Were Americans inadvertently stumbling into an environment with stricter rules? The questions buzzed in the air like an electric current, providing tension but also fueling deeper dialogue.
